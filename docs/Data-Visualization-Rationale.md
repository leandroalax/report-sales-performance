# Data Visualization Rationale

## Purpose

This document explains the rationale behind the selection of each visualization used in the *Sales Performance Analysis Dashboard*. The goal is to ensure clarity, analytical coherence, and effective communication of insights to business stakeholders.

**Purpose:** Provide an automated, high-level summary of key findings.
**Rationale:** This visual allows decision-makers to quickly understand major trends, extremes, and comparisons without manually exploring each chart.

## Pie Chart – Sales by Segment
<img width="532" height="302" alt="pie chart" src="https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/e32cbb57-897e-4492-ade2-ae54b3d9da44" />

**Purpose:** Show proportional contribution of each segment to total sales.
**Rationale:** Pie charts are suitable for categorical data with few categories, making it easy to compare relative participation.

## Bar Charts – Sales by Manufacturer and Category
<img width="700" height="317" alt="bar chart" src="https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/96672534-c543-4702-8fa7-f74117e83970" />

**Purpose:** Compare absolute sales values across manufacturers and product categories.
**Rationale:** Bar charts support precise comparison between categories and quickly highlight top and bottom performers.

## Map Visualization – Sales by Region and Salesperson
<img width="1287" height="716" alt="Print - Vendas x região e vendedor" src="https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/bbfd7c1b-5202-4a40-b038-f6aace8bb0da" />

**Purpose:** Display geographic distribution of sales and individual performance.
**Rationale:** Maps are ideal for spatial analysis, helping identify regional concentration, coverage gaps, and high-performing territories.

## Design Considerations

* Preference for visuals that balance analytical depth and executive readability.
* Consistent color usage to avoid misinterpretation.
* Focus on business questions rather than purely aesthetic choices.

## Conclusion

The selected visualizations collectively ensure that the dashboard supports both exploratory analysis and executive-level decision-making, translating raw sales data into clear and actionable insights.
